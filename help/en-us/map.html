Finding signals
  • Filters: In the Filters tab, use Add filter (e.g. Signal type, Line code).
  • Legend: Click a category to isolate matching signals.
  • Network ID: Filter by a specific ID, then click the pill label to zoom to the signal on the map.
  • Line code: Filter by code or line label, then click the pill label to fly to the line's extent on the map. Hovering a pill previews its geographic extent on the map as a dashed outline with a dim overlay — the preview clears as soon as the map moves.
Map interaction
  • Controls: Right-click a signal for the context menu or use the keyboard shortcuts below.
  • Visibility: All signals appear at zoom 10+. Below this, a spatial sample is used to maintain readability (active filters still apply).
  • Persistence: Filters, pinned signals, map position, and settings are saved across sessions.
Hover tooltip

Hovering a signal marker opens a tooltip. Each row shows the signal type (colored label) and its Network ID. When co-located signals differ in direction or placement, they are split into sub-groups separated by a divider. Line code, track code, track name, and milepost are always shared by co-located signals and appear at the bottom.

OSM presence indicators appear once the app has checked the area against OpenStreetMap:

IndicatorMeaning
Dotted underline on Network IDThat specific signal is already mapped in OSM
OSM logo on the first rowAt least one signal in this group is already in OSM

The check runs automatically after the map has been still for 30 seconds at a sufficiently zoomed-in view (zoom ≥ 14, fewer than 100 signals visible), or immediately when hovering a marker.
